How our pure-water process works

Traditional window washing pushes soapy water around the glass and leaves residue that streaks in the sun and attracts new dirt. We work differently. Our system filters water down to zero dissolved solids — no minerals, nothing to dry into a spot — and feeds it up a water-fed pole to a soft brush that scrubs the glass, frames, and sills.

Because the water is completely pure, it pulls dirt off the glass and dries crystal clear on its own. No soap film, no water spots, no streaks — guaranteed. It also means we safely reach second- and third-story windows while standing on the ground, so there are no ladders leaning against your gutters or siding.

Inside your home, it's old-school care: drop cloths down, hand tools, microfiber detailing on every pane, and sills wiped before we move the furniture back exactly where it was.

Hard water stain removal in Columbia

Those cloudy white spots that won't wipe off? That's not dirt — it's mineral deposit. Sprinkler overspray, roof runoff, and well water leave calcium and magnesium baked onto the glass, and once it etches in, ordinary window washing just polishes the haze. This is one of the most common calls we get from Columbia homeowners, and it's a specialty service we've built a dedicated process for.

Fresh mineral spotting usually releases with our pure-water wash alone. For older, set-in staining we step up to a dedicated hard-water treatment: a glass-safe mineral dissolver worked in by hand, followed by a fine polish and a purified-water rinse. The result is glass that's genuinely clear again — not just cleaner haze. We'll always show you a test pane first so you can see the difference before we do the whole house, and if a pane is etched beyond saving, we'll tell you straight instead of charging you for a miracle.

  • Sprinkler and irrigation overspray stains — our most common Columbia request
  • Well-water and roof-runoff mineral streaks
  • Glass-safe dissolvers and hand polishing — never harsh acids or scratching pads
  • Free test pane so you see the result before committing

The best times of year for window cleaning in Columbia

Spring (April–June): This is the big one. Connecticut's oak and pine pollen coats every south- and west-facing pane in Columbia with a yellow-green film, and screens catch even more of it than the glass does. A late-spring cleaning — after the pollen drop finishes — resets the whole house for summer.

Fall (September–November): Our second-busiest season. Summer thunderstorms leave mineral spotting, and cleaning before the holidays means bright rooms for every gathering — plus you head into winter with sealed, clean tracks instead of gritty ones.

Before listing your home: Ask any realtor — clean windows are the cheapest renovation that exists. We do pre-listing cleanings across Columbia on short notice, and sellers consistently tell us it changed how bright every room photographed.

After construction or painting: New builds and renovations leave stickers, overspray, and dust that ordinary washing won't touch. Our post-construction detail work handles all of it safely, without scratching new glass.
